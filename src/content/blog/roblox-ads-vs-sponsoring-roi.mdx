Paid promotion works when the numbers add up. Here’s a fast, practical model to decide when **Sponsored Experiences** and other Roblox ad formats return a profit.

## The quick ROI model

> **Break-even** happens when the **cash you DevEx from the new players your ads bring in** is **greater than** what you spent in **USD**.

Now the compact version:

- **Spend (R$):** your promotion budget in Robux. In Ads Manager, credits sit near the DevEx bridge (≈ **285 R$ ≈ $1**).
- **Cost per play (CPP, USD):** what one new player costs you. Recent benchmarks: **$0.007–$0.011 per play**.
- **Conversion to payers (%):** share of new players who buy something.
- **ARPPU (R$):** average Robux a payer spends in your experience.
- **ARPU in R$ (effective):** `conversion × ARPPU`.

**Break‑even test (USD):**  
`(New Robux from campaign × 0.0037975) − Spend_in_USD > 0`

Use **$0.0037975 per R$** as the DevEx factor. Prefer an automated version? Try the [/calculator](/calculator).

## Two promotion paths (what you’re buying)

- **Sponsored Experience (platform placement).** Buys plays right now at a known CPP. See Roblox help: [Sponsored Experiences](https://en.help.roblox.com/hc/en-us/articles/206455923-Sponsored-Experiences) and the latest [Ads Manager updates](https://devforum.roblox.com/t/ads-manager-updates-acquire-new-users-continuous-campaigns-and-more/3862159).
- **In‑experience ads (Immersive/Rewarded Video).** Brands pay, you earn Robux per impression or view. Docs: [Immersive ads](https://create.roblox.com/docs/production/monetization/immersive-ads).

## Scenarios

### A) Low spend test: 5,000 R$ (modest conversion)

- **Spend:** 5,000 R$ ≈ **$17.54**
- **CPP:** **$0.009**
- **Expected new plays:** ≈ **1,949**
- **Payer conversion:** **0.5%**
- **ARPPU:** **300 R$** → **ARPU per new player:** 1.5 R$

| Inputs                                        | New Robux earned | Net USD after DevEx | ROI verdict       |
| --------------------------------------------- | ---------------: | ------------------: | ----------------- |
| 5,000 R$ (~$17.54), $0.009 CPP, 0.5% × 300 R$ |     **2,924 R$** |          **$10.23** | **Fail** (−$7.31) |

### B) Medium campaign: 20,000 R$ (average conversion)

- **Spend:** 20,000 R$ ≈ **$70.18**
- **CPP:** **$0.008**
- **Expected new plays:** ≈ **8,772**
- **Payer conversion:** **1.0%**
- **ARPPU:** **350 R$** → **ARPU per new player:** 3.5 R$

| Inputs                                         | New Robux earned | Net USD after DevEx | ROI verdict        |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------: | ------------------: | ------------------ |
| 20,000 R$ (~$70.18), $0.008 CPP, 1.0% × 350 R$ |    **30,702 R$** |         **$107.46** | **Pass** (+$37.28) |

### C) Influencer/UGC tie‑in (higher‑intent traffic)

- **Spend:** **$150** influencer fee **+ 10,000 R$** sponsor boost (~**$35.09**) → **$185.09** total
- **Effective CPP:** **$0.006**
- **Expected new plays:** ≈ **30,848**
- **Payer conversion:** **2.0%**
- **ARPPU:** **400 R$** → **ARPU per new player:** 8.0 R$

| Inputs                                                | New Robux earned | Net USD after DevEx | ROI verdict         |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ---------------: | ------------------: | ------------------- |
| $150 + 10,000 R$ (~$35.09), $0.006 CPP, 2.0% × 400 R$ |   **246,784 R$** |         **$863.74** | **Pass** (+$678.65) |

**Read:** When the audience matches your game and offers are tight, creator promos often outperform platform ads. **Risk:** mismatch kills conversion. Start small.

## Sensitivity check (±20%)

Using Scenario B as the base:

| Effective ARPU per new player (R$) | Net USD after DevEx | ROI verdict        |
| ---------------------------------: | ------------------: | ------------------ |
|                     **2.8** (−20%) |          **$85.97** | **Pass** (+$15.79) |
|                     **3.5** (base) |         **$107.46** | **Pass** (+$37.28) |
|                     **4.2** (+20%) |         **$128.95** | **Pass** (+$58.77) |

**Takeaway:** ROI hinges on monetization. Improving **D1 retention** and shop design often matters more than shaving CPP by a fraction of a cent.
